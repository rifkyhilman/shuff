The passion or passion for life that is embedded within some people doesn't always seem to be able to appear on the surface because for one reason or another they decide to keep it to themselves even though that passion may be something they really like and are interested in.

Several factors such as job demands, the economy, personality, or even physical conditions are some of the things that prevent some people from deeply embracing their passion. However, as we know, opportunities are always there and can come at any time, including to awaken the passion within us, whether it is passion in a hobby, dream job, or a dream that has not yet been realized.

Understanding Passion


Before we discuss further about how to awaken passion in ourselves, let's look at the definition of passion itself below! According to the Big Indonesian Dictionary (KBBI), passion means passion, enthusiasm, great desire, joy, emotion, anger and anxiety. Basically, the concept of passion is not ambition, desire, dream or wish. However, passion is something that begins with awareness. Passion is a person's desire to do something or an activity that he likes or considers important. Those who already have passion are willing to do various things to achieve something. In fact, they are willing to use their time, energy and thoughts to achieve something.

This is something we consider a passion. So if you find something or an activity that makes you ready to switch things up in your life, it means you've found a passion that you enjoy. According to Vallerand and Houlfard, passion is a strong tendency to perform pleasurable actions.

How to Awaken Passion in Us


Finding or identifying passion is not easy. In fact, it often takes a long time. Even for years. There are many cases where people do not know their passion until they are alive. Apart from encouraging you to do things with passion, we also discuss how to develop it.

Here are some ways to awaken passion in us :

  • Find the motivation behind the decision
  • Discover your values and what you believe in
  • Look for patterns and themes in the answers to the questions you ask yourself
  • Don't commit yourself, commit to “ready but” behavior
  • Also think about what activities you have always wanted to do

This fact shows the importance of passion in a career. Before you enter the world of work, you must recognize your passion from now on. This will make it easier for you later when you graduate and finally enter work, because working with passion is much more enjoyable than working without passion.
